Anew Web App

From student loans to mortgages to credit cards, debt is an unavoidable component in the financial ecosystem. A good majority of people own what's called 'good debt'. However, for those struggling with 'bad debt', options for help are few. So for many, managing debt becomes irrevocably tied to stress, anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues.

Role

Research, competitive analysis, product design, design systems, usability testing

Scope

Solo project (design challenge)

Tools

Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Usability Hub, Optimal Sort, Overflow, Miro

My process

Research

To start off, I decided to investigate the common pain points that indebted users experience when trying to manage their debt, and did so through user interviews and surveys.

Ideation

The challenge: design a cross-platform debt and money management web app that helps users track their repayment progress, build a safety net of savings and improve their financial wellness.

Wireframe

I started with some simple mockups of the landing, dashboard and goal tracking pages, and used them as a frame to get people's reactions to the anew concept.

Testing

Now that I had a basic prototype in my hands, the next logical step was to put it to the test in hopes of discovering points of friction that I might have missed, and opportunities for improvement.

Design

How can we create a design system that promotes mindfulness without sacrificing professionalism and trustworthiness? To start, I conducted a quickfire round of competitor research, looking at fintech apps alongside wellness apps to help inform anewâ€™s design system.

There were so many qualitative takeaways from this project, especially from the user research and testing sessions. I enjoyed listening to everyone's stories and emphathising with their pain points. As a designer (and a bit of a perfectionist!), I realise it's easy to get so sucked into the details of the work that I forget the impact it can have on the people I'm designing for.

This project brought to light how important it is to design for not only functionality, but positive change. It's moments like these that remind me why I'm a user experience designer!
